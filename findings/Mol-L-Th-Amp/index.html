Description
L-Theafetamine is a novel dual-acting prodrug consisting of D-amphetamine covalently linked to L-theanine through an amide bond. This construct is inactive until enzymatically cleaved in vivo, releasing both stimulant and neuromodulatory components. The design delays CNS activation, reduces dopaminergic spikes, and provides anxiolytic and neuroprotective effects.

Applications include treatment of ADHD, narcolepsy, depression, and cognitive fatigue. The prodrug offers reduced abuse potential compared to traditional stimulants.
Synthesis
The synthesis strategy couples D-amphetamine (Formula I) with L-theanine (Formula II) to yield L-Theafetamine (Formula III). Key steps include:
• Preparation of a protected L-theanine intermediate (benzyloxycarbonyl group preferred).
• Amide coupling with D-amphetamine.
• Deprotection under hydrogenation to yield the conjugate.
• Crystalline intermediates enable purification by crystallization.

The method achieves >99.9% purity even from low-purity amphetamine starting material, making it scalable for pharmaceutical manufacturing.
Pharmacology
• Dual release: stimulant + neuromodulator.
• Cleaved by red blood cell amidases, plasma esterases, and hepatic peptidases.
• Modulates dopamine, glutamate, and GABA pathways.
• Adjustable release kinetics via linker chemistry and formulation.
Salt Forms
L-Theafetamine and analogues can form pharmaceutically acceptable salts, including hydrochloride, sulfate, phosphate, tartrate, fumarate, and citrate salts. Salt forms aid in stability, solubility, and formulation versatility.
